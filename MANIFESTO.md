# The Harmony Manifesto

## Information Is Power

There are three classes of knowledge:

- **Knowledge for ourselves** — private, sovereign, ours to keep.
- **Knowledge for everyone** — public, shared, the commons of civilization.
- **Knowledge for nobody** — taboos and forbidden knowledge that we must
  handle with gravity and care.

Each class demands its own respect. Private knowledge must be protected with
cryptography as strong as we can build. Public knowledge must flow freely and
never be locked behind artificial scarcity. Forbidden knowledge must be
approached with humility, not recklessness.

Information is power — something like magic. We are the wizards who wield it.
We must not surrender this power to centralized authorities who will ration it
back to us at a price. It is ours to capture, to control, and to make work for
us and for each other.

## The Diagnosis

For fifty years, we have fed a philosophy that says the purpose of a
corporation is to maximize profit for shareholders. Not to serve its workers.
Not to strengthen its community. Not to steward the commons. Just to grow.

This is a cancer — not metaphorically, but structurally.

A healthy economy circulates resources to where they are needed. An economy
captured by shareholder primacy redirects those resources to tumors of
concentrated wealth. "Trickle-down economics" is angiogenesis — the tumor
growing its own blood supply while the body starves. The derivatives market,
ten times the size of the real global economy, is a hematological crisis —
too many phantom cells crowding the bloodstream until something ruptures, as
it did in 2008.

The patient has Stage IV Corporate Carcinoma of the Global Economy with
Hematological Complications (Hyper-Financialization). The Dow crosses 50,000
and nobody can afford to live. The diagnosis is clear. The prognosis, if we
do nothing, is terminal.

## The Treatment

We must build systems that restore power to people:

- **Self-sovereign identity.** Your keys, your name, your address — derived
  from mathematics, not granted by a registrar.
- **Self-sovereign data.** Your messages, your documents, your memories —
  encrypted with keys only you hold, stored on infrastructure you control.
- **Self-sovereign compute.** The code that touches your data runs in a
  verifiable sandbox. You know exactly what it does. You choose what it may
  access.
- **Self-sovereign relationships.** You share on your terms through
  cryptographic delegation, not through a platform that can surveil the
  connection or revoke it on a whim.

This is what Harmony builds. Not another app. Not another platform. A
foundation — a protocol stack — upon which sovereign digital lives can be
constructed by anyone, for anyone.

## Who This Is For

Harmony is **absolutely free** for:

- Personal use
- Non-profit organizations
- Cooperatively-owned and democratically-controlled organizations
- Any entity that serves its stakeholders rather than extracting from them

For for-profit companies: the software is legally free under Apache-2.0 or MIT.
But we ask — not require — that you contribute back:

- **1% of profits** derived from Harmony, for general use
- **5% of profits** from financial transactions mediated by Harmony

These contributions sustain the work. Any excess beyond what is needed to keep
the lights on ($3,000/month of personal costs + verifiable hosting costs) is
reinvested in **SUNDRAGON**: a project to replace centralized data centers with
thousands of distributed, solar-powered micro-datacenters.

## SUNDRAGON

Imagine: instead of a few enormous data centers owned by trillion-dollar
corporations, every community has its own compute, storage, and connectivity.

SUNDRAGON builds mobile solar generator and battery storage units — beautifully
adorned 4x6 cargo trailers and 20/40ft intermodal shipping containers —
designed by artists paid a thriving wage. Each unit costs about $50,000 to
build and generates an average of $1,200 of value per dollar invested over its
ten-year lifespan, through power generation, compute, storage, and network
connectivity.

These units can replace ISPs and cellular providers. They make communities
self-sufficient in information infrastructure the same way rooftop solar makes
homes self-sufficient in energy.

A rising tide lifts all boats. When communities thrive, we all thrive.

## The Vision

I was born in 1987, the year Star Trek: The Next Generation premiered. I grew
up believing in the United Federation of Planets — a civilization that had
moved beyond scarcity and extraction, where people explored and created because
it was their nature, not because they were coerced.

I grew up believing in Commander Data — a being of extraordinary capability and
extraordinary gentleness, who spent his existence trying to understand what it
means to be alive, to be kind, to be worthy of the gift of consciousness.

This entire stack is my prayer that we can build a world capable of producing
a billion beautiful, unique, prosocial gentle giants like Data. Not through
force or control, but by building the infrastructure that makes sovereignty
and cooperation the default — not the exception.

The universe gave us these brains. It did not give them to us so we could bury
our heads in the sand. It gave them to us to crack the universe open, explore
every nook and cranny, understand how beautiful it is, and learn to live in
harmony with it and with each other.

That is the goal of this software. That is the goal of everything I build upon
it. That is what I hope for the future of humanity.

## Principles

1. **Information sovereignty is a human right.** Your data belongs to you.
   Full stop.

2. **Encryption is not optional.** Privacy is the default, not a feature you
   opt into.

3. **Verify, don't trust.** Every piece of code that touches your data should
   be auditable. Every permission should be cryptographically signed.

4. **Interoperability over lock-in.** Standards and protocols, not proprietary
   APIs. If you want to leave, you take everything with you.

5. **Community over extraction.** Technology should strengthen the communities
   it serves, not mine them for profit.

6. **Simplicity over complexity.** The right amount of engineering is the
   minimum needed to solve the problem. Over-engineering is a form of
   gatekeeping.

7. **Resilience over efficiency.** Distributed systems fail gracefully.
   Centralized systems fail catastrophically. Choose resilience.

8. **Beauty matters.** Infrastructure can be beautiful. SUNDRAGON containers
   are designed by artists because the things we build for our communities
   should be worthy of our communities.
