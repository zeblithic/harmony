# ZEB-136 TinyLlama cross-arch findings

**Date:** 2026-04-17 (oracle extraction + matrix executed; report finalized 2026-04-18)
**Runs on:** AVALON (RTX 5080, 16GB VRAM)
**Teacher:** TinyLlama/TinyLlama_v1.1
**Branch:** `zeblith/zeb-136-tinyllama-cross-arch` (with `origin/zeblith/zeb-134-skip-to-logit` merged in to unblock router-on cells)
**Spec:** `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-04-17-tinyllama-cross-arch-signal-design.md`

## Headline verdict

**Both Δ-diffs < +0.001** → matches verdict-matrix row 3:
*"Cross-arch at 40M frozen is fundamentally hard regardless of gap.
Smaller gap didn't help. ZEB-138 (teacher-match via same-arch Harmony
teacher) becomes the decisive test. ZEB-137 pretraining remains on the
critical path because ZEB-138 depends on its output."*

Crucially, this verdict is *cleaner* than the spec anticipated: TinyLlama
oracle outperformed Mistral on every health metric, so Risk A
(teacher-weakness confound) is *ruled out*. The result is not "smaller
gap didn't help conditional on teacher quality being high enough" — it
is "smaller gap with a *better-quality* teacher still didn't help."

The router learns to emit *uniform* logits over the 32K vocabulary

oracle-agnostic — content-blind by construction at convergence.

This is a *bit-for-bit* phenomenological match to KRILE's ZEB-134
Mistral baseline.

### Verdict mapping (forensic rubric)

- **Not (R) RETRIEVAL-BROKEN**: (D) ≈ 14% is healthy, (E) ≈ 0.63 is
  selective softmax (well below uniform 1.0), (P) sim-gap ≈ 0.04 is
  moderate (above the ≲0.05 collapse threshold).
- **Not (I) INJECTION-BROKEN**: (M) at L2 = 0.048 is material; cross-run
  cos = +0.873 (L2) / +0.807 (L5) is *very* high — the opposite of (I)'s
  "orthogonal directions across runs" prediction.
- **Not (I\*)** for the same reason.
- **Not (I\*\*) FIXED RESIDUAL-AXIS AMPLIFIER**: (W) RMS_cos ≈ 0.09
  (need ≳0.9) and (A) |cos| ≈ 0.04 (need ≳0.5). V is *not* a fixed
  amplifier on the residual axis.
- **Not (V-CONTENT-BLIND)**: (X) |cos| ≈ 0.20 is well above the
  random-pair floor of 0.066 (≈3× the floor), indicating V responds to
  retrieved content rather than emitting content-independent outputs.
- **Match: (D\*) DISTRIBUTIONAL ALIGNMENT** — the post-2026-04-17 W/A
  refinement: low within-run R (0.147 / 0.091) means per-token directions
  span diverse axes within a run, but the *distributions* of those
  directions align across runs (cross-run cos +0.87 / +0.81). V is
  token-sensitive; the content-invariance is a trained alignment of
  response patterns, not a fixed projection.

(Q-overlap) Jaccard ≈ 0.001 separately confirms per-token retrieval is
content-*dispersive* (the 14% marginal breadth is genuine per-token
diversity, not a Q-projection artifact). (V-rank) v/e = 1.00 → V
preserves the retrieved subset's effective rank, no V-side compression.

This is the *same* (D\*) fingerprint observed in KRILE's η-B Mistral
forensic on 2026-04-17 — replicated bit-for-bit with a smaller-gap,
higher-quality teacher.

## Joint reading: oracle health + Δ-diff + forensic + router

The four signals tell one consistent story:

1. **Oracle is strong** (PCA 0.934, |cos| 0.152) — content reaches the
   table in usable form.
2. **Retrieval is healthy** (D 14%, E 0.63, content-dispersive Q) —
   content reaches V's input.
3. **V responds to content** ((X) above floor) — but the response
   distributions trained-align across oracles ((D\*) cross-run cos 0.87).
4. **LM is blind** (Δ-diff +0.000224 / +0.000244, router emits max
   entropy) — V's content-aligned distributions deliver no
   discriminative signal to the next-token loss.

The **content-invariance localizes downstream of V**, not at the
retrieval or injection mechanism, and not at the teacher's hidden-state
quality. Reducing the architectural/capacity gap from 175× to 28× and
upgrading oracle PCA fit from 0.88 to 0.93 changed *nothing* about this
phenomenology. The bottleneck is not the gap.

## Verdict

**Verdict-matrix row 3 — confirmed unambiguously.** Cross-arch at 40M
frozen is fundamentally hard regardless of gap. ZEB-138 (teacher-match
via same-arch Harmony teacher trained in ZEB-137) becomes the decisive
test for whether this is an *architectural* incompatibility or a *frozen
backbone capacity* limit at 40M.

## Open questions / surprises

- **TinyLlama oracle outperformed Mistral on every health metric.**
  Spec's Risk A (TinyLlama undertrained, weaker hidden states) was
  overcautious — TinyLlama-1.1B v1.1 produces *more* PCA-compressible
  and *less* directionally-collapsed hidden states than Mistral-7B-v0.1
  on this corpus. This is a useful finding for any future cross-arch
  experiments: gap and quality are independent axes, and TinyLlama is a
  legitimate cross-arch teacher, not a degraded one.
- **Engram contribution is ~5 millinats over 2000 steps**, indistinguishable
  across all 4 cells. Frozen backbone + zero-init gate makes the engram
  path effectively inert for 2000 steps regardless of what we feed it.
  Worth checking whether longer training + warmup eventually opens the
  gate and whether *that* surfaces a Δ-diff (deferred to ZEB-138).
- **Skip-router landed within 5% of KRILE's Mistral baseline on every
  diagnostic.** α, ‖W_align‖, entropy, even cross-run cos all reproduce
  the prior numbers nearly exactly. The bypass-decoder hypothesis is
  *not* gap-sensitive — the router learns the same noop mapping no matter
  the teacher.
- **(D\*) replicated bit-for-bit.** The 2026-04-17 W/A refinement that
  identified distributional-alignment as the failure mode is now
  validated against an independently-collected oracle. Strong evidence
  the diagnosis is correct — the redesign target is real.
